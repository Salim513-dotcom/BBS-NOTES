What is Research Design?

Definition: Overall plan of the research in which problem is defined and solution is written properly in a format for data collection, research method, analysis, process and procedures of the research.

üìñKey Definitions

1. C.R. Kothari

"Research design is the conceptual structure within which research is conducted. It constitutes the blue print for the collecting, measurement and qualities of data."

2. F.N. Kerlinger

"Research design is the plan, structure and strategy of investigations conceived so as to obtain answer of research question and control variance."

3. Modern Definition

"Research design is the written plan, structure and strategy of investigations to solve a research problem and control variance by using modern methodology of research."

üìà2. Developmental Research Design

Definition

A research is called developmental if it used to predict the upcoming trend considering the changes of the events, human, social and cultural activities. Normally, it tries to follow the trend of the changes of the past, present and future.

a. Longitudinal Growth Study

Researcher studies an event or problem in different time periods to answer the research question. Data collection is done in different time periods caring the dependent and independent variable.

Key Characteristics:

  • Sample may be same but time must be different
  • Situation must be different but issue must be same
  • Measures rate of changes and development
  • Studies different situations in different intervals

b. Trend Study

Researcher tries to find trend of an activity or event in a certain interval of time to predict the future depending on the past and present situation by the study of the frequency of the studied activity.

Example: Design of clothes is changed according to the fashion of the people adapted in the locality or society by the tailor.

c. Cross Sectional Growth Study

Researcher divides a field area into different groups and questions are asked to the respondent at the same time in their respective groups in the frequency of day, week or month.

Method: Done on the basis of the survey using different men for different groups at the same time.

d. Cohort Study

Research done among similar people's group having similar character, behavior, age, knowledge, professional norms and situation.

Example: A survey for the Officers selected by PSC of Nepal on 2075.

üß™Experimental Research

What is Experimental Research?

A research is called experimental if researcher really observes different phenomena which is made to occur in a strictly controlled situation where one or more variables are valid and others are kept constant. Has aim to see the relationship between variables and formulating hypothesis.

Key Characteristics:

  • Can be done in laboratory or work field
  • Commonly done in science and its branches
  • Takes much time and expenses
  • Field works done in social science and business
  • Verification done in deductive research
  • Synthesis done in inductive research

Steps for Experimental Research:

1

Review literatures related to research problem

2

Identify problem properly

3

Define problem after identification

4

Formulate practical plan:

  • Identify factors influencing relationship
  • Find affecting factors for controlling variables
  • Select research design
  • Select sample and respondents
  • Select tools and test validity
  • Predefine procedure for collecting data
5

Formulate null hypothesis

6

Reduce data to draw expected result

7

Provide result using appropriate method

üìèBasic Principles of Research Design

What are Principles?

Principles are the normal fundamental assumptions of the research. We have to follow at least these norms in the real practice.

‚è∞ 1. Principle of Timeliness

Researcher has to specify the time for research and must complete it on the specified time.

üîÑ 2. Principle of Flexibility

Research design and real field work may be different in real practice. Expected and real field may be slightly or highly different and researcher may get problems so design must be flexible to complete it.

üéØ 3. Principle of Objective

Researcher must make the research design properly to make it specific and objective removing the different unclear views and concepts.

‚ôªÔ∏è 4. Principle of Replication

Researcher must make a research design in such a way that next one can copy it in next purposes and next context to make a theory.

üîí 5. Principle of Reliability

Researcher has to make a research design in such a way that the result for the same sample using the same tool must be same. We say this type of design is consistent and the result so obtained is also consistent.

‚úÖ 6. Principle of Validity

Research design should specify the variables that can measure the things what they want to measure. Validity is integrity of the conclusions that are generated from a piece of research.

üåç 7. Principle of Generalizability

Research design must be made in such a way that the result obtained for the sample should be true for the whole population and the other cases except the taken sample too. We must be able to generalize the finding of the research to the normal cases also.

‚úÖCriteria of Good Research Design

What Makes a Good Research Design?

Good research design depends on utilizing the optimal methodology and study type for the research question. Good research design does not happen by chance. A good design must contain whether it is related to the purpose of the research problem and its nature or not.

üéØ 1. Objectivity

A research design should permit the measuring instruments which are fairly objective in which every observer or judge scoring the performance must precisely give the same report.

Note: The objectivity of the procedure may be judged by the degree of agreement between the final assigned to individuals by more than one independent observer.

üîÑ 2. Reliability

Reliability refers to consistency throughout a series of measurement which is repeated many times.

Example: A respondent must give the same answer even if the question is asked many times from different people.

‚ú® 3. Feasible

Good design can be implemented in practice. The designed matters must be easy to the researcher and respondent as well. The tools used and the other things regarding the research must be made easy to execute it depending upon the time and expenditure.

‚úÖ 4. Validity

Good design is called good from the side of validity if it can measure what it is expected to measure.

Example: If we want to measure intelligence of a person by I.Q question then it must be measured by asking the question.

üåê 5. Situational

Research must not be rigid. We must be able to change it when we work in field or laboratory. Works must be done depending upon the situation also. So it is made flexible to modify depending on the developed conditions.

‚ö° 6. Efficient

Good research must balance the uncertainty and try to make all of them in balanced condition. It should be less costly and include strategies for solving potential threats that may occur while conducting the research.

üåç 7. Generalizability

Research is done normally to generalize the case. Researcher takes the sample and verification is done within the sample first. If it can be generalized to the whole population and other cases then only it is supposed nice.

Requirements for Generalizability:

  • Instrument must be objective, reliable, and valid
  • Population is clearly defined
  • Appropriate sample is taken
  • Proper analysis of statistical tools are followed
