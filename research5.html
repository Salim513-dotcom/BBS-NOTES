‚ùìQuestionnaire

Definitions

Wallace & Wallace: "A questionnaire is means of gathering information by having the respondent fill in answers to the printed questions."

G.A. Ludberg: "Fundamentally the questionnaire is a set of stimuli to which people are exposed to answer on the printed question."

General: A systematically standardized set of questions to collect responses from respondents on specific topics or issues.

Questionnaire Helps To:

  • Get information from respondents
  • Get answers from respondents
  • Get behavior of respondents
  • Get understanding of respondents
  • Get psychological aspects
  • Gain conceptual understanding of issues

üìÑComponents of Questionnaire

üé§Research Interview

What is Interview?

N.H. Gopal: "Interview is conversation with a purpose and therefore is more than oral exchange of information."

General Definition: Face-to-face purposive conversation to get information, interest, attitudes, behavior, and perception of the person related to research or concern issue.

Features of Research Interview

  • Must be purposive to obtain objective
  • Sufficient time should be given for full information
  • Appropriate and easy words and language used
  • Capacity of respondent must be considered
  • Interviewer should listen carefully
  • No directions given to respondent while asking
  • Interviewer must have art of interview

üëÅÔ∏èObservation Method

What is Observation?

A method of primary data collection where the researcher observes, analyzes, and interprets the event, field, or work personally without using any medium.

Researcher reaches required field to make visualization, listening, reading, and keep records in suitable way. May touch, read, watch, smell, measure, etc.

Why Observation is Considered Best:

  • Direct personal experience
  • Real-time data collection
  • Multiple sensory inputs
  • Contextual understanding
  • Reliability increases with real-based cases

üî¨Inferential Statistics

Parametric Methods

  • Estimates relationships of variables in population
  • Sample size > 20
  • Has normal distribution
  • Examples: Mode, Linear Regression

Non-Parametric Methods

  • Data is ordinal
  • Sample size < 20
  • No normal distribution
  • Limited calculation power
  • Examples: Chi-square test, Median test, Binomial test
